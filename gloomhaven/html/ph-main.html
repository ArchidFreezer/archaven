Plagueherald

The class's playstyle is what would be called a 'debuffer,' which is someone who specializes in reducing the effectiveness of enemies or putting debuffs (negative conditions) on enemies that make your allies more effective. The Plagueherald does the above through the use of Curse and Poison. Curse is one of the more controversial conditions in the game as large amounts of Curse can lead to trivialization of scenarios by significantly reducing enemies' ability to deal damage. We don't really care what other denizens of Gloomhaven think of Curse though, we're pretty clearly an evil bug-creature and we're out to destroy our enemies and consume their corpses, so we're more than happy to take advantage of the power of Curse-stacking.

We are a squishy ranged class, so our survivability is sometimes an issue, but we also have a large hand size of 11, which means we can afford to lose a card to negate damage when necessary. While not technically a damage-dealer class in terms of style, many of our attacks are actually a bit overtuned to the point that we get our debuffing more or less for free, meaning we do actually bring a fair amount of damage to any party, even before you consider the absurdity that is Baneful Hex at level 7.
