Blinkblade Tactician

The Tactician build's core mission is to extract maximum value from the Blinkblade's core Time Token mechanic: you want powerful explosive 'Fast' turns and effective 'Slow' turns that minimize 'Slow' downsides.

The Tactician focuses on three things to maximize value:

  • Hit as many targets as safely possible with multi-target effects (and ensure you can take 'Fast' turns at a good time to do so)
  • Using Wounds and inflicted damage to eliminate targets that your attacks are ineffective against due to effects like Shield and Retaliate.
  • Plan your 'Slow' cards in advance to ensure you minimize the cost of those turns.

    • So why would you play a Tactician Build?

    • Tactician has the most freedom in its choices - you can tailor your exact Tactician build freely to your party composition and the type of enemies you expect to fight during your current story arcs.
    • Tactician isn't focused around any particular loss synergy, so will have the highest effective stamina among the builds and the most freedom to play loss cards in each scenario to fit their situation.
    • Tactician will reward careful players who enjoy planning and adaptive tactical thinking the most of the builds - it's the most suited to players who want to treat Frosthaven combat coupled with the class mechanics as a puzzle and try to solve it.
    • Tactician is effective at adapting to enemies that Blinkblade generally struggles with. If a scenario is about Shields, you can tailor your hand to battle against Shields. Same for Retaliate, Wound immunity, or any other wrench the scenario may throw into your plans.

    The Tactician build loves the following:

    • Are Drifters: Drifter is also excellent at adapting to varied situations, and a strategically minded Drifter + Tactician Blinkblade party will find themselves able to tailor their plans to effectively combat absolutely anything the scenario throws at them.
    • Are happy to Heal or take hits for you. The more your allies can take pressure off of you, the more focus you can have on maximizing damage output.
    • Wound or Poison enemies - especially if they do so efficiently or to many enemies at once.
    • Benefit from random unused elements or Push effects.
    • Strengthen or Regenerate us at key times when we move into combat or out of it.

Top Loss: So I've said a lot of words about 'weakness to Shields' and here we go. One card to fix all our problems, right?! Weeeelll… kind of. There are subsets of enemies that this just absolutely deletes. It's one of the strongest losses in the game if you play it in a scenario where every enemy has 3+ Shield. Bottom: This is another card that's situational, though it can frequently be triggered in higher player counts. At lower player counts, it's going to be fairly difficult to trigger and often won't be the card you want. If you can trigger this safely, the reward is quite significant – especially on 'Slow' turns, where it allows you to exceed 2 Time Tokens and start stockpiling.

Phasing is generally good for higher player counts because it gives you a powerful option against Shield-heavy scenarios while having a generally useful bottom action.

Fast: This card's top action is a bit awkward for 'Fast' turns, since it's significantly stronger if you can afford to spend Time Tokens, and is fine-not-great if you can't. Most of the payoff is gated behind the Time Token usage, so unless you've managed to get a stockpile of those, this should be used for its bottom action during 'Fast' turns. The bottom action is very useful for helping certain types of allies, but needs allies that are close-by and want the granted Move. In a team with something like Bannerspear who cares about lining allies up in position, or movement-challenged allies who need the help, it can shine. Alongside ranged allies who stay quite far back, it can be difficult to get more than your Move 4 from it.

Slow: NOW we're talking. For SlowBlade, this is outstanding because the cost of a Time Token is relatively low, and it grants them much needed exp. They can also use the 'Fast' side of it a lot more freely because they don't mind taking multiple 'Slow' turns in a row. The initiative on both halves is middling, so be sure to pair it with cards that will allow you to weave your initiative effectively. The bottom part of this can be very good for some allies and not particularly good for others - you'll know if you're in a party where your allies really need this large a granted movement.

Rushed or going down levels is generally good for lower player counts, since the top of Rushed competes for our precious Time Tokens. If you have trouble keeping Time Tokens stocked and/or have few allies who need granted movement, just go down to level 5 or 6 to pick up a solid card from those levels. SlowBlade has a slam dunk pick here and doesn't really want Phasing under most circumstances. (unless you were certain you were about to enter multiple shorter-length scenarios filled with Shielded enemies).
