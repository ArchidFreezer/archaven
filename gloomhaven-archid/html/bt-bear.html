Beast Tyrant Bear

This build is for the bear build where the Beast Tyrant does not engage in combat directly, instead commanding the bear to perform attacks instead.

There are a couple of implications for this, the first is that you tend not to pick up as much loot as you are not moving with the battle and standing on coins as you go, the second is that there are some scenarios where the complete party need to travel to exit hexes in which case you need to keep Disorienting Roar in your hand that lets you swap places with the bear.

Foundation Card

The core strategy of this build is the use of the persistent loss bottom of the Concentrated Rage card. This allows us to give up the top or bottom action of a card to improve the bears turn.

We typically do not want to be giving up the bottom of our cards for the move, preferring sacrificing our top action for an additional +3 attack for the bear.

There are two implications for this for our strategy

  • We want bottom command attacks where possible so we can combine these with the sacrificed top for multi-attack rounds.
  • Some cards we only want for the initiative so we don't care what the actual card actions are as we will be forgoing them anyway.

    • The way this works is that we get to forgo the top and/or bottom of our selected card for the round to get the corresponding bonus for the bear. This bonus is then applied to both the bears auto-action, plus any command action from the card which we did not forgo. If we forgo the top action of our card the bear will have a +5 attack and then an additional +3 to any command attack on the bottom of our selected card. This is why we are looking to have command attacks on the bottom rather than the top.
