Blinkblade SlowBlade

This build is one where we usually choose to go slow which means that token management is less of an issue for us as we usually have 2 available for when we want a 'Fast' turn

The key is the top loss on Precision Timing that adds +1 to all attacks when taking a 'Slow' turn. We then take cards at levelup to play into this maximising the number of attacks we play on 'slow' turns.

So why would you play a SlowBlade Build?

  • SlowBlade deals the most sustained damage of all three builds unless an enemy is heavily Shielded. What you trade away in tactical options you gain in raw damage.
  • SlowBlade can still take effective ‘Fast’ turns as needed, and almost always has spare Time Tokens to choose on a round-by-round basis what speed they want to play.
  • SlowBlade rewards players who enjoy brute forcing their way through the scenario - you don’t need to worry as much about planning multiple turns ahead to avoid having the wrong cards on a ‘Slow’ turn. Instead, ‘Slow’ turns are your most powerful ones!

The tank build loves the following:

  • Allies who can go early and lock enemies in melee attacking someone other than SlowBlade
  • Allies who can rescue you or Heal you up if things look bad because you took too much damage or had an unfortunate enemy ability flip while you went very late in the round.
  • Allies who are highly effective against Shielded or Retaliating enemies.
  • Allies who can Strengthen or Regenerate them at key times when they move into combat or out of it.

Supplementary

Four cards remain which we’ve identified as falling outside of our ideal ‘Core’ cards. At level 1, we get to bring one of these. At higher levels, we’ll still bring them sometimes if the scenario looks correct for them or our party composition heavily supports them.

Sap Speed is a powerful, but narrow “Fast’ Attack with a very hard bottom action to use and a poor initiative for most uses. This will be our level 1 default in higher player counts, if no other card is a good fit for a scenario, or otherwise against swarms of non-retaliating enemies.

Hit and Run is another powerful, but narrow ‘Fast’ Attack with a poor initiative, though its ‘Slow’ action is also decent if our party can benefit. If your party can easily avoid an Immobilized target’s attacks, this will usually outperform Sap Speed. In parties that tank for you, it combos well with Temporal Displacement for ‘Fast’ double Attack 3 + Immobilize, though that combo’s initiative is so bad that you don’t want to run it without support.

Cascading Reaction is our go to if we need more good Movement actions if, we need quicker initiatives, or we think we will combo its top Attack loss into Sand in the Hourglass to deal with shielded or retaliating enemies. Keep it in mind even for later levels where initiative, Movement, or Wound are going to be very relevant in a scenario.

Overdrive is very narrow. You should not use this to try to tank unless you’re doing it for fun or memes. The only uses of this card that I don’t consider ‘just for fun’ / suboptimal is a.) In scenarios filled with high Retaliate enemies with low or no Shield value, where its use is obvious, or b.) In scenarios or parties where you NEED the jump to get around smoothly. Both uses are rare, but may still come up at higher levels. If you see the right time, don’t hesitate to take the card. When it’s good/valuable in a scenario, it’s quite important to bring.

‘Fast’ and Aggressive: The Assassin

‘Fast’ 02: Open with a Borrowed Time Move and Blurry Jab on the best target you can reach safely (in 4p, this may be an enemy in back!). Use your Poison Dagger with the Jab.

‘Slow’ 74: Do a triple Attack via Twin Strike + Temporal Displacement (can use Sap Speed bot if you have that) and use the Poison to get an effective Attack 2+3+3 despite being ‘Slow’

‘Fast’ 17: Play Power Leak on two adjacent enemies and then Heal the poison off or get to safety with Sand in the Hourglass or a similar bottom action. Alternately, if you need a Move to use your Target 2, Move up to two enemies engaged with an early-initiative ally so you won’t take too many hits.
Note: Avoid being the target for a group of 2+ enemies for all builds anytime you’re Poisoned! Stay safe! The more hits you take, the less aggressive you can be later!

‘Slow 99: Play Kinetic Transfer to Move 1, Attack 2 Range 2 and Drive Recharge to Heal our Poison off and Regen some of the damage we took.

Next round, try to finish off whatever’s left while preserving your Regen leading into a long rest so you’ll be ready to start the next rest cycle in fighting shape.

An all-in super-high damage opener for parties that can support some shenanigans from their Blinkblade. This will put a huge pile of damage into the first room immediately, but involves serious risk taking and will get you killed if you don’t have allies that can support you effectively.

‘Slow’ open: Let ‘em come

‘Slow’ 99: Let the enemies Move to you. Kinetic Transfer top Move 1 Attack 2 Range 2 to reposition yourself adjacent to two enemies. If you don’t need the Move, Play Drive Recharge bot to Heal initial damage and put Regen on to cure your future Poison.

‘Fast’ 02: Play Power Leak top for two Attack 4’s and then Move to safety to preserve your Regenerate so it can clear Poison next round. We are glad for Borrowed Time’s 02 here!

‘Fast’ 24: Play Twin Strike Top + Temporal Displacement bot to triple Attack, distributing attacks among anything left alive and avoiding disadvantage on the ranged Attack if you can. If in danger, instead use the Top Move to get to safety and skip the Top Attack 3.

From here, try to preserve Regenerate and engage the rest of the room safely. Ask allies to support you or tank for you as needed. Don’t forget to use your Poison Dagger effectively!

This opening sequence is for rooms where you can rely on (lightly or un-Shielded) melee enemies closing with you round 1. Line yourself up to take 1-2 light hits in the first round if you can, but only if it’s safe. Communicate to your allies that you’ll make Ice if they need it.

Facing Shield/Retal Groups

‘Fast’ 12: Play Kinetic Transfer’s bot Move right into the middle of the largest pack of enemies, followed by Experimental Adjustment Top to put a pile of non-attack damage into the enemies while going Invisible. Move to a hex that disrupts enemy movement if possible.

‘Slow’ 71: Play Blurry Jab’s Bot Move 2 + 1 dmg. For the top action, Hit and Run is listed – but you should only use it if the immobilize or attack will have value. If you didn’t bring it or attacking won’t help, just don’t perform a top action. Late initiative to avoid attacks.

‘Fast’ 02: Play Sand in the Hourglass Top to Wound and damage a Shielded enemy, along with Borrowed Time (or any good movement with early initiative) to run to safety.

Finish the rest cycle by putting whatever attacks you can into any good targets while staying safe until you rest back your non-attack damage cards. Depending on enemies, you may want to rest early. Keep Overdrive in mind if the enemies are high Retaliate but un-Shielded. If you have Overdrive, run its bottom action with Twin Strike Top after or before this pattern.

This combination is built to punish enemies you don’t want to attack individually.

Facing a Shielded Tank

‘Fast’ 20: Play Twin Strike’s bot Move 3 Pierce 2 Wind and move to the Shielded enemy,
Play Blurry Jab Top Attack 4 Wound to get the pain train rolling.
Consider Poison Dagger on this attack if other allies will hit the enemy.
Note: it’s often ideal to take a hit here since you’ll Heal during your 'Slow' turn.

Play Drive Recharge Bot to Heal 2 Regenerate and start recovering

This opening sequence works for any room with a high-hp Shielded enemy. Ask allies who can apply Wound to put it on something (ideally another Shielded target) during the first round. Mention that you’ll generate wind if anyone needs it.

New Cards

Top Loss: Dare to dream. This is an absolutely amazing tempo boost, allowing two top actions (generally) during your next four turns. The output of this card can vary wildly, but you’ll gain 4 full top actions worth of tempo from it as long as the damage doesn’t kill you - making it quite a powerful loss option to have. NovaBlade’s entire mission is to maximize the interaction between this and Double Time. They’ll have some incredibly fun and explosive Nova rounds (and will pay the price in stamina and health to do so). Read more about this loss in that guide, but keep the loss in mind for other builds - it does work if used carefully.

Bottom: Teleport 5 is a great movement option under every circumstance. This card is especially amazing as 'Slow' movement as long as you’re playing carefully enough to afford the hit point loss. Unless you’ve used Teleport, it’s easy to underestimate how much more versatile it is than even a Jump Move. You can entirely dodge out of rooms and leave enemies needing to travel 10 hexes or more to get to you, or take multiple non-movement turns and catch up to the party in an instant if the map tiles are aligned for it. The initiative is also excellent, even for ‘Slow’ turns (comparably), and can do a lot to help us stay out of harm’s way. Though we will often have immunity to Immobilize by this level, it’s important to note that Teleport works whether or not you Immobilized yourself.

I put a lot of value on superior initiatives and cards that let me engage enemies from more positions. I also put a premium on cards that allow me to take powerful actions during ‘Slow’ turns. Breakneck Speed checks all those boxes and I just love the card.

Fast: ‘Consume an element for Attack 4 Target 2 Range 3’ is the part of this card you want to play in almost all situations. If you’re taking this card, you’re taking it intending to play it ‘Fast’, consuming an element, and targeting 2 enemies. The other modes of this are significantly weaker - but this mode in particular combos quite well with various effects. Novablade enjoys getting an Attack 4 Target 3 Range 5 from it, SlowBlade likes using it with Systems Reboot to get Attack 5 Target 2 Range 3, and Tacticians like the option to AoE effectively from range. If your allies can’t help you with elements, line this up following Twin Strike Bottom or Kinetic Transfer Top during prior turns.

Slow: This is… fine. It’s an ok weaving initiative, and, if you can consume the element, it’s a two-target Attack 3. Using this for its 'Slow' action is generally not the place you want to be, but be sure you’re doing the elemental consumption if you do. Even a SlowBlade is better served not trying to use the melee side of this card and should instead aim to combo with Systems Reboot to use the ranged Attack.

Bottom Loss: A powerful-but-situational loss, particularly if you care about the Time Tokens (non-SlowBlade). If it’s going to prevent you from losing a card to damage, don’t be afraid to fire it off and reap the benefit of an influx of Time Tokens to allow you to go 'Fast' for multiple turns. It can often be net-stamina-positive for the party to set the enemies up so you can fire this off for a 4+ target Stun (Do this carefully! Don’t lose cards to set up a loss!), following it up with all-in aggression. This loss gets new life at lvl 8 alongside Fastest Alive for an incredibly brutal combo.

Many Tacticians in less melee-focused parties will find themselves able to get great usage out of the multitarget ranged Attack from Flurry’s ‘Fast’ side.

Top Loss: So I’ve said a lot of words about ‘weakness to Shields’ and here we go. One card to fix all our problems, right?! Weeeelll… kind of. There are subsets of enemies that this just absolutely deletes. It’s one of the strongest losses in the game if you play it in a scenario where every enemy has 3+ Shield. And it does stone nothing to enemies without Shield. So it’s quite situational, and two of our three builds already are planning some losses.

Bottom: This is another card that’s situational, though it can frequently be triggered in higher player counts. At lower player counts, it’s going to be fairly difficult to trigger and often won’t be the card you want. If you can trigger this safely, the reward is quite significant – especially on 'Slow' turns, where it allows you to exceed 2 Time Tokens and start stockpiling.

Phasing is generally good for higher player counts because it gives you a powerful option against Shield-heavy scenarios while having a generally useful bottom action.

Fast: This card’s top action is a bit awkward for 'Fast' turns, since it’s significantly stronger if you can afford to spend Time Tokens, and is fine-not-great if you can’t. Most of the payoff is gated behind the Time Token usage, so unless you’ve managed to get a stockpile of those, this should be used for its bottom action during ‘Fast’ turns. The bottom action is very useful for helping certain types of allies, but needs allies that are close-by and want the granted Move. In a team with something like Bannerspear who cares about lining allies up in position, or movement-challenged allies who need the help, it can shine. Alongside ranged allies who stay quite far back, it can be difficult to get more than your Move 4 from it.

Slow: NOW we’re talking. For SlowBlade, this is outstanding because the cost of a Time Token is relatively low, and it grants them much needed exp. They can also use the 'Fast' side of it a lot more freely because they don’t mind taking multiple ‘Slow’ turns in a row. The initiative on both halves is middling, so be sure to pair it with cards that will allow you to weave your initiative effectively. The bottom part of this can be very good for some allies and not particularly good for others - you’ll know if you’re in a party where your allies really need this large a granted movement.

Rushed or going down levels is generally good for lower player counts, since the top of Rushed competes for our precious Time Tokens. If you have trouble keeping Time Tokens stocked and/or have few allies who need granted movement, just go down to level 5 or 6 to pick up a solid card from those levels. SlowBlade has a slam dunk pick here and doesn’t really want Phasing under most circumstances. (unless you were certain you were about to enter multiple shorter-length scenarios filled with Shielded enemies).

Fast: While turning nulls into crits is reasonable and might help us feel safe, it’s not actually absurd by-the-numbers unless you’re able to manipulate your deck somehow and don’t have a good way to get Strengthen. The attack is functionally around an Attack 6, which is fine – but we’ve got better. The best part of this action is the Move 3 + Invisibility, which gives us a slew of good tactical options, allows us to perform some of our riskier plays with relative impunity, and makes it fairly easy to string initiative weaving together and entirely avoid attacks. With that said, if your deck has 3+ curses, use this Attack.

Slow: Much like the ‘Fast’ side of this card, this bottom action is where a lot of the power lies. Being able to have two rounds of Strengthened attacks on demand is absolutely massive for a class that delivers piles of attacks using an excellent attack modifier deck. This attack is functionally about an Attack 4, so it’s just unimpressive compared to our other good options. SlowBlade especially will benefit a lot from lining up the bottom Strengthen alongside cards like Stab Them All or Twin Strike… and even SlowBlade isn’t a huge fan of the top Attack.

Quantum gives you some tactical options you just aren’t getting elsewhere. If your party members are giving you Invisibility or Strengthen or tanking for you super effectively, you don’t need Quantum. If they aren’t, it can really expand your tactical arsenal.

New Cards

Top Loss: This can be hard to evaluate, but it’s generally better than most players would think. Consider the stats of some good Boneshaper summons: lvl 5 hp 8, Move 2 Attack 3 Wound, lvl 9 hp 6 Attack 3 Range 3 Move 3.. It’s actually quite common to find a level 5-7 enemy with stats equivalent or better than those - and it’ll flip ability cards rather than just Move +0, Attack +0. It also uses your amazing AMD deck and can proc your active area +2’s! The best targets for this card are significantly better than the baseline for a level 9 summon. So, if you find a great target - use it! Besides that, it’s level 9 - aren’t you meant to have fun with them? Slowblades should note that if your first monster dies and this loss will still good, you can Systems Reboot the loss back and clone another enemy!
Note: If you play on Scenario level 6-7, this loss becomes quite powerful: you should generally use it anytime you see a good target, and try to keep it alive.

Slow: Even for level 9, this is exceptional mobility - and like Breakneck Speed, it only asks an hp cost to give us full power during ‘Slow’ turns. Teleport also gets significantly better the further above Teleport 4 you get, frequently allowing you to move between rooms entirely, leaving even the furthest moving long-range enemies unable to reach you.

The perfect pairing for a situationally powerful loss is a generically powerful non-loss.

Top: This is an incredibly strong ability for ‘Fast’-turn-focused Blinkblades, particularly NovaBlades, whose card choices happen to synergize with this ability. It is also at its absolute best during Nova turns, where it triples up on its effect. Some mind-blowingly amazing target damage come from using this ability with Breakneck Speed and Double Time active. It’s level 9, so if you’re a NovaBlade - just have fun with it! Figure out the best possible combinations, line up all your available buffs and just go wild. See how much damage you can deal a boss in a single round, and be sure to tell your party about it every single game session from then on. ;)

Slow: The value of this teleport varies wildly depending on the scenario objectives and how far you are from the beginning room, but is always optional… and the rest of the card is outstanding. In just about every scenario, this is an important stamina-extending healing option to keep in mind. It gets you back your two best cards, enabling all sorts of combo nonsense, particularly for NovaBlade, who gets to run their combo twice! (probably the second best combo to return is Flashing Flurry bottom when used alongside Fastest Alive top).

This card can produce huge damage in the right combinations.
