Tinkerer Damage

Overview

The Tinkerer is the support class of the starting 6. The Cragheart can also be somewhat of a support but the Tinkerer is the one true support option you start with. She has great healing and a medium-sized health pool. What she lacks, mostly, is damage. She has a number of attacks, some of which are quite decent, although many times they're good because of status effects rather than the damage they deal (further solidifying her role as a support). Her few really good damage attacks are all losses and unlike the Spellweaver, she can't reuse them. Unlike any other of the starting classes, however, she has a hand size of 12, which is enormous and both gives her a ton of longevity as well as the flexibility to use more loss cards than most other classes. She typically plays from ranged, as she has little benefit from getting into melee range, but a reasonable health pool and a huge hand size means she can risk getting up close and taking a few hits if absolutely necessary.

There are really two paths to take with the Tinkerer when you level her up: focusing on healing or focusing on damage-dealing. This guide will be for the damage build that is better for 3p group as the group may still need some support and the Tinkerer will bring just the right amount by focusing on the damage build. With the healing build, you may find yourself lacking damage when your group needs it more than healing.

Build Deck

New Cards

Adjacent heal; we are not going to be going into melee range so nothing to see here even though the strengthen is a nice bonus in round one.

Big move loss. We don't really need big moves to get in the faces of enemies as a support.

Energizing Tonic... The initiative is the only good thing about this card. The top is a reasonable heal with strengthen, but we aren't a class that has good melee attacks to use this on ourselves. The bottom loss is unnecessary. Don't play this card.

The top is your standard level 1, attack 3, range 3, which will make it something you want to use often in order to help finish enemies off.

The bottom is also pretty useful. At the very worst it can add 1 attack to your ranged attacks when you don't need to move, and if you have other ranged allies or occasionally melee allies, it can add to their attacks as well. While it doesn't always do a lot, it will consistently add 1-2 attack, and sometimes more, making it a valuable bottom action.

Enhancement Field is one of your hard-working staple attack cards.

The only shame is the bad initiative, which does mean it needs to be paired with a faster card (conveniently Toxic Bolt is perfect here) in order to get the buff up before your allies go.

Flamethrower has the highest damage potential of your level 1 loss attacks. Wound is usually at least 1 damage meaning this is effectively at least attack 4 on each target, making it higher damage than Ink Bomb. It's worse, however, because it requires you to get into melee range to use it. That's worse not just because it can open you up to damage, but also because it limits your options for a bottom action, normally requiring a move.

At the same time, the bottom of this card is much worse than the other 2 loss AoE attacks as it's much less frequently useful. There are times it can be used, but it's situational.

The first level 1 AoE loss attack we'll look at for the Tinkerer, Flamethrower.

This card has both a situational top and bottom. Obviously the high upside of Flamethrower still makes this not terrible, but it's far below the other two much better overall AoE cards we have.

If your group lacks a solid tank, there will be plenty of times when you'll want to bring out this summon to absorb some damage for a fight or two.

I wouldn't overly fixate on the top though because the bottom is the best part of this card.

If bottom attacks are good for damage-dealers, bottom heals are just about as good for a healer. There are plenty of times when you won't need to move or will really need to heal and having a bottom heal action is great. 3 range makes it easy to not have to worry about not being able to move. You'll also be able to use this to remove poison and land a bigger top heal.

Harmless Contraption is both a good and funny card. I love the summon flavour-wise, although mechanically it's pretty mediocre, the shield does provide a bit of longevity for it.

The only real downside of this card is the bad initiative, which is risky on a heal, but you'll just need to pair it with something faster, of which you have plenty.

A top Move and Loot is situational, but something we can always use. That small enhancement dot next to the Move is very interesting as well as a top move 2 is very nice when moving between rooms given that we are usually towards the back of rooms.

Bottom attacks are always useful and this can be used to ground a flying enemy and then potentially pull them into a trap. The damage is fairly low.

Hook Gun has a situational top on a spammable bottom which is a combination we can get behind. Both sides are fairly average, but combined we can make use of them.

The top loss will normally be able to target 2 enemies, making it an attack 8, 1 behind Fire Orbs. Still, being 1 attack behind the best level 1 Spellweaver loss isn't such a terrible place to be.

There will occasionally be situations where you can hit 3 enemies with this, at which point it's insane attack for a level 1 card. Creating a dark doesn't really matter much but is just a small added bonus (and occasionally negative).

The bottom is the move 4 we LOVE to see on a good top loss. Now we'll have access to a great move card up until the point we find the right moment to use our loss attack.

Ink Bomb, a good big loss attacks the Tinkerer has access to.

Everything about this card is amazing except the initiative, which is the only thing holding it back.

It loses 1 attack on each target to immobilize. Obviously, power-wise, that's an upgrade, although it does add a situational effect rather than a generically-useful effect like attack. This loss attack therefore lends itself more towards supporting and less towards damage-dealing, as you'll prioritize using it when the immobilize matters in order to stop incoming damage. It's still an excellent loss attack.

The bottom move is also powerful, if also again not as generically-useful as just a move 4. Still, there will be plenty of situations when the immobilize the move will stop an enemy from dealing damage for a round, which makes this great.

Ink Bomb, a good big loss attacks the Tinkerer has access to.

Everything about this card is amazing except the initiative, which is the only thing holding it back.

The top is no better than a loss attack for the damage build.

The top loss has positives and negatives. The negatives are that you can't control when this triggers so you need to be careful with your healing plans afterwards to not accidentally waste charges on overhealing (you really need to get at least 8 healing out of this to make it worthwhile).

The positive is that you can combine this with AoE heals and since it's 'next four Heal actions', it will affect the entire action for only one charge. At level 1 this would be your move 2 heal 1 to all adjacent allies, turning it into a heal 3 on multiple targets. Not the easiest to pull off with a move 2, but it is doable and even just healing 2 people for 3 with that is great value. Boots that add movement can also help with this combo if you're going for the healing build.

The bottom is a move 3, which is, as always, neither great nor terrible. Still, it's something we can spam while we wait for the right moment to set up the top loss.

A pretty average card for a damage build, not giving us much we need. 46 initiative is also very average.

The top is fairly terrible. The funny thing is that this might be good against enemies with high shield as a source of direct damage but most enemies with high shield actually fly, making this truly useless unless you use one of your grounding cards.

Fortunately, the bottom is a move 4, and we'll need some of those, so we may end up using this regardless.

Proximity Mine - oh yay, another trap.

The top has a synergy with Hook Gun bottom as we can ground a flying enemy and pull them into a trap. The problem is that both these card have bad initiatives so we will likely be going after the enemy has attacked which is not good.

The top action is fine, heal 3 range 3 is what a standard good heal should look like at level 1.

The bottom loss, on the other hand, is more interesting.

It's reasonably-powerful, although not great. The biggest problem is that the card has a greater effect the sooner you use it, but the sooner you use it will also have a more painful effect on your longevity. Still, there can be a happy medium and some classes with small hand sizes (like the Scoundrel) can really benefit.

Conveniently, the Scoundrel has 9 cards, which means that whether you use this her first time or her second time through his deck, it will give her the same number of extra turns. So we can use this on round 4 or 8, which gives us more flexibility while still retaining the maximum effect from the loss.

Reinvigorating Elixir, the first of the Tinkerer's many healing cards we'll see. This is just an overall average card that you'll be neither happy or unhappy to have.

And that brings us back to this card, which thus becomes a good loss card by having a (mostly) spammable half as we can expect someone to be taking some damage between rests.

The card also has decent initiative.

Heal 3 range 3 is a standard heal on top and welcome.

Move 2, heal 1 affect all adjacent allies is also a good bottom. Combines well, as mentioned, with your loss healing buff. Also removes poison effectively, allowing you to do this bottom and a heal on top that will actually get through.

Otherwise, it's just a small amount of free healing on top of a move action for rooms where you don't need to move much.

Restorative Mist is to healing what Enhancement Field is to attacking.

And on top of all of this, it has great initiative. One of your best cards, by far.

The top is a bit ahead of the curve for an average attack card at level 1, normally we'd expect 3 attack but this one will give us 4 attack if we can find 2 targets within range 3. Not terribly difficult to do, although it's much worse against shield, even small shield on enemies like guards or skeletons. Still, the upside is usually going to be worth it at early levels.

The bottom is much worse. A non-loss heal is heal 3 range 3 at level 1 and this is effectively 1 number higher (although 2 healing higher) for a loss. It is a bottom heal though, and sometimes a big heal now is what you need and the 4 range should let you get it to where it is needed most. Still, while it's always good to have the option, it's quite rare that you'll find yourself needing or wanting to use this loss.

Reviving Shock is another pretty good card.

The initiative is fine if not excellent.

The top is another support attack, not really dealing any damage but being able to stun an important target. Having 20 initiative means you'll usually be able to stun the enemy.

The bottom is a move 4, which is always good.

A good top and a good bottom with good initiative makes a great card.

Also, as an early enhancement suggestion, consider adding wound to this card. Wound has great synergy with stun, effectively adding a minimum of 2 damage and also making this card a good answer to high shield, low hp enemies.

Attack 2 range 3 poison is great for a level 1 card. It will be worse than attack 3 about 1/3 of the times and better 2/3 of the time, meaning this is ahead of the curve for a level 1 card. And it creates an earth, which isn't important for you but can help a fellow Cragheart or Spellweaver.

The bottom attack is rarely worth using as a loss but there may be occasions when it will let you finish off a tough enemy, summoner, or boss and you'll use it. The fact that the top is so good means you'll always have the option around and pay nothing for it.

Now Toxic Bolt is a card! Great top, good initiative and a nice situational bottom.

op actions that create traps aren't really very good at all. You have to do a fair amount of work to just do 2 damage and poison, which is a lot worse than just attacking for the same effect. Obviously there are some tricks you can do with traps to abuse enemy AI but situations where it's worth it are pretty rare and thus it's not really worth considering playing this half of this card.

Meanwhile, the bottom is not much better. At its base we trade an action and a card for a card for someone else and we have a short range restriction in order to even get to make this bad trade. 'Filler' (for situations where you're not actively fighting) actions like this on bottom are also rarely useful because we can usually just make filler moves to pick up loot. So we need someone who makes ice but doesn't use it, which isn't normally a thing, and even then we get an action that's barely worth it.

Well, Volatile Concoction is a terrible card. Let's just forget this card exists, we won't be missing anything.

New Cards

The top is a crazy effect (not necessarily because it's that powerful but it just does a crazy sort of thing). It is, though, a very powerful and very situational effect. Just over 1/4 of regular enemies in the game will be able to be killed undamaged with this. Some scenarios there won't be any and some scenarios there will be a lot. On scenarios where this card is good, it can single-handedly win you the scenario. It is an amazing answer to all those little annoying enemies with high shield and low hp. And it can still work on other scenarios some of the time when your allies damage enemies.

Disarm on a non-loss move is sooooo good. Disarm is functionally stun against a large portion of the enemies in the game, and getting a free disable on a bottom where you still get to move is amazing. With this plus your stun attack you can remove 2 enemies from attacking for a round. I cannot possibly overemphasize just how good this effect is. You will also really appreciate having boots that give movement with this card.

Disintegration Beam is a really good card.

The great thing about this card, though, is that while the top is situational, the bottom is pretty much always amazing.

The top is a reasonable effect that can be powerful enough (especially with certain items) to make it worth not attacking or healing. That being said, it is still situational.

The bottom is a powerful loss AoE attack that can definitely be worth using, but is also situational.

Noxious Vials is a good card.

So this card has two powerful but situational effects, which is not ideal but the effects can be powerful enough that it may be worth bringing.
