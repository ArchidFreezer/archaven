Sawbones Tank Assist

Saw has two defining conditions when building him: do you have a tank in your party or not? Accordingly, we will have two builds. Why is that so important? The persistent losses Booster Shot and Research the Cure, which are some of the best persistent-losses in the game, really only work with a tank. The best part about these cards is that you can be the difference in the party between a class that can 'kind of' tank on its own to become a full-fledged tank. Some classes in the game have their own tank builds that don't quite have enough support within the class itself (classes like Mindthief and a few unlockable classes), but throwing an additional permanent Shield 1 on top will usually make the difference between wonky and truly effective. And obviously if you throw that Shield on top of someone who's naturally very tanky, you can end up with some of the most broken combos in the game. Furthermore, what's amazing about this is you don't need to become 'a support' in order to have this effect - you just have to play a single persistent loss, which is realistic enough to do as you otherwise have great non-loss actions and a 10-card hand size, so you can certainly afford the one early loss per scenario.

This build is for when you have a tank in your party.

Build Deck

New Cards

Saw has some really powerful losses, even at level 1 with things like Booster Shot and sometimes Vaccine, so the bar is quite high and this largely falls short. Why? A bunch of reasons:

  1. Healing losses are typically the worst losses in Gloomhaven. Healing just isn't something you normally want to do as a primary action in combat because it's basically just putting a band-aid on huge gash - you help with the symptoms, but you're not addressing the problem that lead to the symptoms, and thus things will just get worse again next turn. Typically, if you're in a bad spot, you're better off using a loss for a big attack or crowd control in order to survive combat and then heal up afterwards. For example, it's almost always better to use an Attack 4, Stun loss than a Heal 6 loss.
  2. Even if you would desperately want healing badly enough to play a loss, this is delayed healing, so it doesn't even immediately address the problem. You have to play this and then wait another turn to just get the effect, which is insane!
  3. Med Packs are a useful mechanic that we'll play with a fair amount on this class but the biggest thing med packs do is giving extra longevity (free turns), whether played for healing or default actions. A large med pack and a small med pack give the same amount of longevity, only one of them actually costs longevity to give.

The bottom non-loss is quite powerful (because immunity to negative conditions removes existing conditions) and is a bonus to movement. The problem is the initiative which means that the immunity comes after most enemy attacks will have happened so we would need to pair it with another lower initiative card to make the card really effective.

Battlefield Medicine starts us off at a pretty low point. This card is simply not good, although it can be powerful on some scenarios. The biggest problem is that it's a loss combined with a highly situational effect.

Attack 3, Wound is a perfectly reasonable amount of melee damage for a class at level 1. For comparison, the Mindthief can do Attack 4 (which is typically worse than this) and the Scoundrel can do Attack 5 but with conditions attached and she only has 9 cards to our 10, thus we're in a good spot with this Attack already.

Bottom Loot actions are generally quite bad (because you can't Move and Loot in the same turn, thus you're better off typically just moving and picking up coins by being on top of them at the end of your turn) but at least this one is attached to a top action that we want every rest cycle so it's pretty free to have it there and will mostly just help in scenarios that require Loot actions.

Bloody Saw, on the other hand, is a very good card.

The initiative is pretty good, although we have some really good initiatives, still this card's initiative will matter because of the combo with Hold Back the Pain (seen later).

First of all, the top Heal is more than reasonable for a level 1 Heal. Typically, we expect Heal 3, Range 3 and here we get 1 more healing at the cost of needing to be adjacent to the target. Fortunately, as we're a melee character who's likely to take a fair number of hits each scenario, many times the target will just be us. That being said, we're not actually going to have this Heal action for very long, as we're only keeping this card at low levels or if we intend on playing the bottom loss, and if we're playing the bottom loss, it's typically the earlier, the better.

The bottom loss is excellent if we have any sort of tank or tanky character in our party. With a Sunkeeper or Red Guard this action becomes overpowered as they are naturally tanky on their own and this is a significant boost to already powerful classes. Thus, it's more if you have a tanky melee ally, use this bottom loss as early as you conveniently can in most scenarios on that ally and watch them (and your whole party) enjoy the significant increase in damage mitigation.

Booster Shot is another great card, although its true greatness will depend on your party.

On top, we get 1 less healing than Booster Shot, but also the ability to remove negative conditions. The initial Poison removal means that the heal actually gets through to the target which makes this much more valuable. The top will still end up being useful outside of combat though, so neither half is bad.

It's often going to be more convenient to simply use this bottom action, which provides you with a 19-initiative Move 3, to remove the condition and instead still get to play a meaningful top action.

Ultimately though, while being a nice card, our deck has a ton of nice cards, which means that beyond early levels, this isn't a card that's going to go into our deck by default. Instead, you should consider bringing it to each scenario on an individual basis depending on the negative conditions present.

It's decent against Poison-rich scenarios and it's excellent against Shaman (who can Disarm and Immobilize), excellent against level 4+ Wind Demons, Spitting Drakes (where you can get Stunned but also they do Muddle so it still does something), etc. So consider the scenario and the enemies and if it seems that there are enough conditions to merit this card, bring it. Otherwise, we've got better things to do.

Giving out a Medical Pack and gaining an xp is a nice action, but we obviously don't want to be doing this in combat - while in combat, you should focus on attacking and CCing. But when moving from room to room, while out of combat, being able to give allies free longevity (and sometimes healing) is great!

Meanwhile, when we're in combat, this card is just a casual Move 4 with 8 initiative. Yeah, I'd say that's pretty good. Our initiatives aren't bad overall, but we're no Scoundrel or Mindthief, so we do appreciate a really good initiative when we can get it, and this is it, as you're never going to be unhappy to 'have to play' a Move 4.

First Aid is excellent. We've got our first med pack giving action, which is nice.

Hand of the Surgeon top compares poorly to Bloody Saw. Attack 4 will typically just be worse than Attack 3, Wound, unless your party already has a lot of Wound (we'll have a lot of Wound eventually but not while it matters with either of these cards).

Meanwhile, the bottom here is more interesting as you can move to whichever party member needs the assistance before using your top heal. You can easily use this bottom with a top Heal to do a lot of healing, assuming damage has been pretty evenly spread.

The initiative is also a lot worse here, as the 60-70 range is typically the worst initiative in the game (neither able to reliably go before or after enemies). That being said, we just don't have that many Attack actions, so even one that's worse than another we already have isn't terrible for us (and Bloody Saw is also a really good Attack, so this being a bit worse still makes this fine, if not exciting).

But first of all, the top action is fine here. If you somehow have this card in your hand while moving from room to room, it will provide a welcome Heal action. And pairing a top Heal (which is good out of combat) with a bottom action that is insane in combat is a great pairing.

So yeah, this bottom action... As I said in the introduction, this class often gets a bad rap as it's misunderstood as being a 'support class.' Does this bottom action look 'supporty' to you?! No, it looks insane! Any melee Attack you want can be a huge AoE! That scales incredibly well with levels and items. And it starts off as just letting you do an Attack 3 Wound on all adjacent enemies with 25 initiative.

It gets truly absurd at level 8. So how should you use this combo? You typically want to try to run in late so that the enemies go before you and you can get into a good spot safely (Hamstring [2] bottom Move is the natural combo), then go early in the next round with this combo. Obviously, you'll need some backup from your allies to help CC or kill some of the targets or you risk taking a lot of damage, but given the power level of what you're doing, they should be happy to help out.

Hold Back the Pain is one of the most dangerous card designs in Gloomhaven and you want to be bringing it every level.

The top is a powerful but situational persistent loss but it's honestly just not that good. In scenarios with negative conditions we care about, we already have Curative Mixture, which does the job much more freely. There's just basically never going to be a scenario where this effect is worth the cost, especially as we already have other good losses with much better effects than this, thus making it really difficult to justify the longevity cost on this one.

Meanwhile, the bottom is very appealing. It's powerful, with good movement and allows us to attack and Wound AoE without the use of HBtP.

This is a solid card with a good bottom action and great initiative.

Non-loss AoE crowd control is one of the most broken things in Gloomhaven (typically only behind non-loss executes - we get both on this class by the way!) We get it at level 1! With great initiative!

There are very few fights in Gloomhaven that aren't made significantly easier by preventing a significant chunk of the enemies from attacking for that round. And we've got enough excellent movement to easily get in position to make the most of this effect.

The bottom isn't very good, by comparison, especially as it would mean not playing the top, but it may occasionally see some use at the very beginning of a scenario, especially when paired with Precaution (a card we'll take at level 3), assuming the layout of the first room isn't conducive to easily using the top. But in general, preventing enemies from attacking entirely > mitigating some of their damage. Take this card, keep it close, spam the top every chance you get, and laugh maniacally at the 25% of people who rated this class as 'underpowered.'

This card has the ability to seriously impact rooms with it's AoE CC along with an initiative that allows us to get of off before most enemies can attack.

The top - giving your tanky ally Shield 2 for the round, assuming that they're going to put themselves in a position to draw most or all of the enemy fire, is excellent at lower levels. That, combined with the bottom of Booster Shot, puts you at Shield 3, which will blank most low-level enemy attacks and has the initiative to get the assists up before either the tank or enemies go.

Meanwhile, the bottom is extremely powerful, giving us hard CC on a bottom action that also adds Poison for free (this would be a fine bottom Attack at just Attack 1, Stun - compare to the Brute's Attack 2 on bottom or the Scoundrel's Attack 3 on bottom at level 1). You can easily move next to an enemy late in the previous round, then go early with this excellent initiative, Stun them, Poison then, and then benefit from the Poison with your top Attack as well (bonus points if your top Attack is Bloody Saw, where the Wound + the Stun here will do 2 extra damage)! Again, with actions like this we're combining good damage and CC, which makes us both a formidable damage-dealer who also prevents damage (the best kind of support).

Syringe is another excellent card, which only seems less impressive because we just got done with one of the best level 1 cards in the game. Neither half is bad, although the bottom is the truly impressive part.

his top action represents recovering a LOT of cards in a large party, especially with summons. The biggest problem with this action is that it kind of exists in Magical Christmas Land - you'll always imagine the best-case, but you rarely get it. In order for this to work, everyone needs to move in a specific way and they also all need to be at ideal points in their rest cycles where this gives back a meaningful amount of cards. Beyond that, this really needs to be done out of combat because of your lack of a combat-oriented top action combined with the movement restrictions for your allies, but recovering discarded cards out of combat is a lot less impressive (because you don't get to replay your best actions). Still, the effect can be impressive, so there's certainly room for the right party to try to build around this action, but on average I'd recommend just forgetting about it and focusing on the powerful and consistent actions you can perform regularly.

While the bottom isn't bad, it's also not great as our nature of being a melee-attacker/CC'er relies on us needing movement more than our allies, thus while technically more powerful than a Move 4, it's typically going to be worse.

Teamwork is a powerful loss, which is refreshing after some misses.

As I already said, Heal losses are typically some of the worst in Gloomhaven and this lives up to that standard.

  1. It's a loss which has no immediate effect!
  2. Because of the Range limitations on the Heal and the fact that we're almost-entirely melee, it's very possible that some turns this does nothing (if we don't need healing).
  3. We typically play losses either for incredible value over the course of an entire scenario on a persistent loss (like Booster Shot) or for a large tempo boost when we desperately need it. This taking place over six turns satisfies neither of those conditions and it's very possible that the last turn or two turns have very meaningless healing as we're out of the tough spot.
  4. Playing the top would mean giving up our excellent Move 5 on bottom, so even in 'last room with plenty of longevity left' situation, we'd still just rather have the bottom.
  5. After all that, the best-case is just a total of 12 healing for a loss (or a bit more if you combine this with Hand of the Surgeon but come on - that's not what you want to do in combat)!

Avoid the top.

But love the bottom! Move 5 at level 1 is really nice as while we have some good movement, we don't mind having a bit more, especially as many of our actions (Hold Back the Pain and Prevention is Key) are position-hungry.

Triage is, on the other hand, NOT a powerful loss.

This top action is so, so, so, so good. Why? Well, first of all, small Attacks that spread Poison to multiple targets are great as you're typically looking at a lot of effective damage from this action. And because Poison gets better the more health the target has (as it will represent more damage), this action scales amazingly with levels and difficulty. Being ranged lets us apply this to high-hp targets (which are typically slow but hit hard) without putting ourselves in danger.

Meanwhile, the bottom loss is also quite powerful, although competes with Booster Shot (and is typically worse). The most common use for the bottom will be on scenarios with a lot of Poison that are also short and where you have a tanky ally (allowing you to put this and Booster Shot on the same ally). Again, that represents a large longevity cost, so the scenario does need to be short.

Vaccine is, I believe, the most underrated level 1 card in the game.

More than anything, more than all the talk about how effective this top action is, the biggest value is in flexibility. Aside from this top action, we are entirely melee. Having a single ranged Attack adds sooooooo much flexibility into how we can still be effective in a huge variety of combat situations. Just ask any Brute how good Spare Dagger is for them - it's not because the action is that powerful, it's just because a single ranged Attack on an otherwise melee class will get an enormous amount of use. And this action is much, much more powerful than the already-excellent Spare Dagger.

Precaution is just a really nice card.

Mmost importantly, with everything we do, this card provides good initiative, and sometimes can even just be a Move 2 with good initiative if we really need it. So while it's not exceptional in any way, it does a lot of little things and accrues quite solid value over the course of a scenario.

First of all, the top can execute elites, although admittedly it's actually a very good design for an execute because it does still require interaction/damage. The top is definitely not the main draw of this card, but you will find some situations where you'll be happy to use it and guaranteed finishing moves can be very valuable.

But the real reason this card is amazing is the bottom. First of all, the bottom adds a ton of damage on its own, easily affecting 2-3 enemies most of the time. But it's not just for this action on its own that it matters, the best part is combining this bottom with the top of Euthanize, which allows us to execute a normal or elite enemy outright, which is quite broken. So each rest cycle, we combine these two to kill any non-named enemy and also apply some bonus conditions to one or a couple extra enemies. Yeah, that'll do.

Fortunately, Grisly Trauma is not underwhelming.

The top loss is kind of nonsense, letting you do healing with non-Heal actions, but we have some free healing anyway on things like Hold Back the Pain and Do No Harm, we don't really need that much healing, especially not at the cost of our level 9 card as a loss. So really, there's almost no world in which this top is appealing.

The bottom, on the other hand, is great. It's a Move 4 with good initiative and a Stun attached. Move + free CC is always welcome and if we took this card, we'd play this bottom every rest cycle and be perfectly happy with it. That being said, it is still kind of underwhelming for a level 9 card, even if it's a fine card and action.

Prep for Surgery isn't a bad card.

Blinking Cape is the best chest slot in the game for Saw, but Saw really wants flying as well (Cloak of Phasing or Boots of Levitation).

If you don't have item Boots of Levitation but you do have item Cloak of Phasing, I would actually suggest taking Cloak of Phasing instead of Blinking Cape, even if you have Blinking Cape. If you have both Boots of Levitation and Blinking Cape, just take those two. If you have Cloak of Phasing but neither Boots of Levitation or Blinking Cape, then Boots of Striding are a better choice than Rocket Boots. But if you have neither 58 nor Boots of Levitation, then Rocket Boots is actually better than Boots of Striding. Finally, Cloak of Invisibility is the choice if you have neither Blinking Cape nor Cloak of Phasing.
