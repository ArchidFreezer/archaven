Spellweaver

The Spellweaver. The starting class designed to fulfil the traditional 'mage' role. At level 1, she focuses heavily on AOE damage with little utility. As she levels, she gains more capacity to provide more than just damage. Of numerous classes with minor summoning sub-themes, she has by far the most effective with 2/2 being quite usable. She also strongly introduces the player to the element board, primarily focusing on Fire and Ice, with a not-insignificant amount of Light as well. She has the lowest hand size of any class in the game but makes up for this drawback with a card recovery mechanic, which primarily allows her to play each of her losses twice per scenario.

In terms of complexity, most of what she does is pretty straight-forward, although the loss recovery system does require a fair amount of caution and planning, which can throw some new players off. Fortunately, she's also more forgiving with playing losses, something new players tend to over-prioritize. She is good against multiple, weaker enemies because of her AOE focus and her biggest issue, by far, is dealing with high-Shield enemies (one of the reasons the Piercing Bow is an absolute must-buy).

Her squishy small hp pool and eight card hand size don't lend themselves to taking many hits. She can cover this weakness a bit with something like an Invisibility Cloak and certainly once she has access to a fully-functional Cold Fire, she's at much less risk, but she does definitely prefer to have someone else in the party who will be closer to the monsters than her.
