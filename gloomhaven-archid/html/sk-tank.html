Sunkeeper Tank

Sunkeeper is an amazing tank, a very solid single-target melee damage-dealer, and she also can bring numerous valuable support tools depending on your group's needs. She does, however, have two weaknesses: she completely lacks top action ranged Attacks, which makes her inflexible in terms of movement/targeting for dealing damage. She also has one of the slowest average rates of experience gain in the game, which is a serious issue for a tanking class as it's important for tanks to remain at or above the average level of their party.

There are two main builds: Damage and Tanking/Mitigation. Focusing mostly on tanking and mitigating damage will be more effective in larger parties and less realistic in a two-player party or in parties with lots of support and little other damage (allies like a Tinkerer). This build focuses on the Tanking/Mitigation choices.

So why would you play a Tank Build?

  • Strong tanking abilities
  • Works in nearly any party

The Tank build loves the following:

  • Loves a Sawbones in the party for the persistent shields.
  • Loves the Plagueherald/Soothsinger combination spreading curses and giving enemies disadvantage.

The bottom is a not-bad loss card. You also have an 11 card hand size, meaning you can certainly afford a couple losses here and there when necessary. This bottom will get more mileage for you at lower levels (where it's also great for free XP) before you have access to more ways to create Light, and you can normally bench it once you can create Light consistently, just bringing it in when you see Poison or Wound as being an4 issue based on the enemies in a scenario.

The top loss can be useful with just a couple other classes in Gloomhaven but most of the time, your average card quality is so high that it's not worth trading a loss card of yours for someone else's.

The heal is most useful for the Light generation if we don't need to move.

A loss on one side require a spammable on the other and healing isn't something we want to be spamming.

While 27 initiative is actually not terrible for this class, it's not worth bringing just for that (especially considering that Move 2's are actually Move 1's for you).

For tanking, until level 7, this is your best top action for when you want to tank a room for your party.

On bottom, Move 3 isn't amazing, especially as it will be a Move 2 for you, but getting to Move 2 with 23 initiative is quite valuable on this otherwise slow class (and initiative is doubly-valuable to us as we want to be able to draw enemy Attacks and move into position before they go).

Cautious Advance is great!

Attack 3 Muddle is a bit underwhelming for an attack, but it's significantly improved by the situational ability to enhance the attack when you can use Dark. This enhancement will pretty much always be done at the expense of Dark created by enemies, rather than allies, as almost any ally who creates Dark will usually want to use it as well. But when it happens, even if it's not often, it feels great.

The bottom is situational but is nice when moving from enemy to enemy as we can pick up loot from one we have just dispatched.

Daybreak is a pretty clever card design, allowing our Sunkeeper, who walks in the Light, to be more effective when darkfriends are present.

The top of Dazzling Charge is the important half and it has a couple things going for it. To begin with, it's a fine Heal, on the same level as starting heals that other classes have, and it's always nice to have a useful out-of-combat top action to play when moving from room to room. The reason it's so helpful to you is the guaranteed source of Light. Now it's not the best source of Light for a damage-dealer because you don't want to have to take a turn off during combat to heal most of the time, but many times it will be worth it. Once you have more options for sources of Light, you can bench this card if healing isn't a priority for your build of the Sunkeeper (based on your party's needs).

The bottom is also not a bad loss. Again, not on the same level as some of your others, but being attached to a good top makes the situational value of it a lot more appealing. There will certainly be times when a Move 5 and Stun, creating a Light to set you up for next turn (towards which end Stun also contributes), will be worth it.

Dazzling Charge is a card that will certainly fit into your hand early on but may not be as important to you later as your deck changes and you gain more access to Light.

The top (which is mostly just there for scenarios with harsh movement requirements that prevent the use of the bottom) is an unconditional Attack 4, which is good enough for a level 1 card. On top of that, if you can spend a Light, you get an Attack 5, 1 XP, and Advantage on the attack. At level 1 this will be slightly more conditional than the Scoundrel's level 1 Attack 5's, but also better because of Advantage. Over time, as light generation becomes easier, it will be significantly better than the respective attacks from the Scoundrel, making you a very solid melee damage-dealer.

Gaining a permanent one Shield is pretty huge in Gloomhaven on a melee character. It's not too hard to make sure you're constantly in front of your party as, lacking ranged Attacks, you naturally want to move into melee range anyway. The amount of damage this action will mitigate in an average scenario is pretty shocking. The drawback here is very real - constantly limiting your movement is a huge penalty and you'll need to work hard to overcome it, but it's definitely worth it. It's also worth mentioning that you can dismiss (move this card from active to the lost pile) this card when you desperately need to move further at the end of a scenario. There may also even be a few scenarios with very harsh movement requirements where you won't want to play this persistent loss, although those scenarios will be few and far between.

Almost no matter the situation, you should pretty much always try your best to play this bottom action on turn 1 of each scenario.

Defensive Stance is THE defining card for the Sunkeeper.

We haven't talked about the biggest deal on the bottom action: the little enhancement dot next to the 'Shield 1.' This enhancement may very well be the most cost-effective enhancement in all of Gloomhaven and should be done as soon as possible. I'd typically buy just a couple items to start (Boots of Striding, Iron Helmet, and a small potion or two) and then immediately start saving to get this enhancement. Shield increases do not scale linearly - Shield 2 is more than twice as strong as Shield 1. Why? Because as you reach higher Shield values, it becomes easier and easier to simply accept incoming Attacks. Accordingly, this enhancement takes an already-powerful action and completely supercharges it.

The top is a Stamina Potion for a friend. It can sometimes even come up that you use this powered-up with Light when moving between rooms, although not really consistently. Still, it's a significant, welcome bonus on an already-great top support action.

And while the top is excellent out of mostly combat, the bottom can be amazing in combat . Attack 4 on bottom on a level 1 card is incredible. Obviously it comes with the caveat that you have to make an ally do it, but as long as you have at least one melee ally, it shouldn't be a problem. With two, it's extremely easy.

Now Empowering Command is a support card.

The initiative is also perfect for this card: early enough that you should mostly be able to go before many monsters, but not so early that an ally can't easily go before you (to get into position).

The top is not very good - a loss for Heal 5, 1 XP is almost never worth it. If you can double it with a Light, it certainly can become worth it, although there's a problem with healing that has elemental requirements. Normally, when you need healing, you need it NOW, not a turn from now. This is because most of the time you want to be killing enemies and not healing, you usually only need healing when something went wrong or when moving between rooms/cleaning up with nothing else to do. If you're moving between rooms/cleaning up, it's not very likely that a) you have the Light to spend, because you probably spent it killing off enemies and b) you want to use a loss card anyway. If something went wrong, it's impossible to say whether you'll have a Light ready or not. Finally, even if you have Light ready and want healing, it's not guaranteed that two different targets will both really need the healing that badly. Thus, while this top can be a powerful heal, it rarely will be.

Fortunately, the bottom is a bit better. Attacks on bottom are always welcome, although this class does have plenty going on for it with bottoms that aren't just movement (and you really need to move with your bottom action most of the time), so it's less necessary than for some other classes. Still, the bottom Attack is fine, and the main thing making this card playable.

Glorious Bolt is just ok.

The initiative is also worth mentioning as this class is pretty slow, thus a 39 initiative, while not great, still sometimes matters at lower levels.

This has the same great top we've already seen on Defensive Stance, which is even better here as it's not attached to a loss on bottom that we might use early in the game, meaning we should always have access to this.

The bottom is a Move 4, which is always good, and especially important for this class.

Hammer Blow is another one of the best Sunkeeper cards.

Take this card, use it often.

The top half of Holy Strike is great, an Attack 2 Stun at level 1 is extremely strong and we like the Light generation.

The bottom half is a situational ranged attack loss card. There will be occasions when it may be worth using this ranged attack, to finish off an important target in a tough fight.

Holy Strike is a tale of two halves.

While it's definitely not one of the better loss cards in your deck, the fact that the top half is so incredibly spammable means you lose nothing by keeping the option around. This is a great example of a good situational loss card. The card is only let down by its terrible initiative for a crowd control card - disables like Disarm or Stun are typically better if you can be sure to go before your target, whereas this will require another card to go early (something you mostly lack). Fortunately, Stun is much better than Disarm when used for the following round as you can disregard the enemy entirely, rather than still having to worry about certain monster deck flips, so it's still almost never bad.

Interestingly, Lay on Hands is at its weakest at level 1, but scales incredibly well with high health pool allies, to the point that you may very well reconsider bringing this card in certain parties at high levels.

Move 2 Bless an ally is great. Often you'll be fighting in close quarters in a room before moving on to the next and you should have the opportunity to move next to an allied melee character and give them a free Bless before making an attack (or attack then move). Obviously this gets significantly worse if you don't have any melee allies as it's much harder for you, as a melee character without top ranged attacks, to Bless a ranged ally and still have an effective top action.

Lay on Hands is a super powerful top loss that gets better as we level.

The card is let down a bit by its initiative, meaning you'll often have to pair it with something else faster or you/your ally will be taking a free attack.

Protective Blessing top provides a lot of value but is difficult to use. Realistically, the only effective way to use this action is if you go last in the round the turn before opening a door, such that you can still use this out of combat but also get the benefit from both the Strengthen and the Bless. When moving from room to room, Dazzling Charge and Empowering Command (seen later) will both typically be better support actions, so you're not desperate for another one of those. And when you're in combat, you really don't want to waste your top action on such a marginal effect.

Meanwhile, the bottom suffers from terrible initiative and being a bottom action. You have to move into the correct spot and also hope to go before the enemies on a class that doesn't routinely break 20 initiative, so that's quite a gamble.

Well, not every card can be good.

There are a few, very specific scenarios where this effect is absolutely worth taking, but otherwise this card is mostly best off being left behind.

The top is a pretty mediocre loss, mostly just good against lower-health, higher-Shield enemies or Retaliate enemies that you can't easily attack. It will never really be a wasted loss because it only triggers when it would deal damage, but your average card quality is so high that it's also just rarely worth playing this loss (although can also be a good way to generate XP, something which is very welcome on this class, in the last room).

Both for your melee Attacks and your tanking, you typically want to move almost every turn (especially as your movement is reduced). Non-Move bottom actions need to be very powerful to be appealing (read: Empowering Command) and this one just typically isn't good enough unless you're in a party with a lot of summons and you already have copious amounts of Light.

There's really only one good thing about Purifying Aura. It's not the top, or the bottom. No, what's really appealing about this card is initiative. At the end of the day, this will most often just be a Move 1 with 21 initiative. That's really not good, but at least until level 3, is often necessary.

The top isn't the half of this card you'll use most often, but it can be useful in a ton of situations, setting up turns and Attacks for your group that wouldn't otherwise be possible, on top of helping your slower allies to move across the battlefield.

Meanwhile, the bottom is another important Move 4, this time attached to one of your best initiatives (and it has a great enhancement dot).

Tactical Order is another really nice Sunkeeper card.

Path of Glory has a great top which is a straight upgrade to your previous versions of this action (Defensive Stance and Hammer Blow). Especially as you typically want to play Defensive Stance bottom from the beginning of almost every scenario, adding another, better Hammer Blow top is great. There's essentially no opportunity cost to have a 'situational' loss on bottom here, but this loss doesn't really seem to be designed around a situation that can possibly arise. For a damage build this is a great card with the spammable attack on top, but with a very situational bottom.

Scales of Justice has a situational yet extremely powerful top loss. There are three real drawbacks to playing this loss: losing a card, losing access to the bottom action, and reducing all of your Attacks by 1. So where should you use this top? On essentially any scenario that involves numerous melee enemies. Retaliate is generally not the best strategy in Gloomhaven, but dumping Retaliate 2 on top of your already-existent Shield 2 is a spicy mixture that functions very well together. If enough melee enemies will Attack you over the course of the scenario, the costs here will easily be worth it. Just be careful that there aren't enemies in the scenario that will make you regret losing access to the Stun (very hard-hitting enemies or summoners).

The bottom is extremely consistent. Light consumption here is purely bonus - sometimes you'll want it, other times you won't need to move far or your boots will be enough. No matter the situation, a bottom Move + Stun action is always incredible and you'll never miss an opportunity to use it.

The bottom here is great for a mitigation card as having a move and stun is incredibly powerful.

Attack 5, 1 XP, and it can Strengthen allies. The only thing that makes it a bit awkward in terms of the Strengthen effect is the initiative timing - how does this work exactly? We'd have to go after an ally, but that would also be after the enemies, which means our ally would need to be adjacent or almost adjacent to where an enemy will be that we can attack after the enemies have gone. That's obviously far from ideal, so most of the time we will need to pair this with one of our faster cards to make use of the Strengthen safely/effectively.

Meanwhile, the bottom is an Attack 6, which is pretty insane, although with a heavy positional requirement. Your group size and composition will determine the effectiveness of this bottom - larger groups with more melee allies will get more use out of this, but even if your group doesn't lend itself to this being very effective, it's still a great option to have in addition to the excellent top Attack.

Supportive Chant is a very powerful support (obviously) card, it works very well with summons who are also considered allies.

The initiative is the other big selling point of this card: as a tank/front-liner, having 11 initiative functions similarly to a 'taunt' effect by allowing you to typically go before both the enemies as well as your allies and make sure you're targeted for the round.
