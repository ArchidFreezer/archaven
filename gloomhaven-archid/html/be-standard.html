Berserker Standard

In this build we play normally, accept that you may occasionally get hit, spend some life to get below half and get some payoffs sometimes and then bounce back above half. Rinse and repeat. This build is going to be the most consistent regardless of party composition or scenario and thus is the one I will recommend here.

The bottom action is obviously excellent because of how good Retaliate is... Oh, no, sorry, it's actually the little dot next to the Retaliate action. Any bottom Move action that has an additional effect where we can enhance Strengthen can be inherently broken and this is no exception with 9 initiative being stellar for this class. So, first of all, why? Well, when you gain a condition, you gain it until the end of your next turn, which means that if we gain Strengthen during our turn, it affects the rest of this turn and the next one. Accordingly, we can use a bottom like this before attacking, have Advantage on all of our Attacks this round and next round. And for a short math preview: with just 6 perks, Advantage adds basically +1 damage to all of our Attacks as well as significantly increasing the predictability (like knowing that we won't miss or we'll most likely draw a +1 or better). Move 2 is rather limiting, but fortunately they make boots for that (more on that later).

Resolute Stand is great. The primary focus of this card is going to be the bottom action and the initiative.

The top is an Attack 3 that you can make an Attack 5 by paying two life. You'll know when that's worth it and when it isn't, and most of the time when it's worth it, it will be the difference between killing an enemy and not, meaning the two life you pay is probably free as it will often negate an enemy attack. And even if it doesn't, you often don't mind spending some life, and getting an Attack 5 at level 1, whenever you want it, is exactly the kind of payoff you're looking for.

Meanwhile, the bottom is also just as good to start and scales even better. Move 4 with 37 initiative is already fine by itself, and added in is just a little more flexibility allowing you to spend some extra life to move a bit further, for when you really need it (the movement or to be at a lower life total).

Strength in Agony is stellar thanks to its two good halves.

This card combines a ton of power and a ton of flexibility and thus is one of your best cards.

The bottom of this card basically just reads 'gain 2 XP' at the end of the scenario as it doesn't do much outside of really silly builds/party compositions and we have much better losses. So the drawback of this card is that it's a top-only with 67 initiative, but the top is just so good that it's not really a problem.

Unbridled Power finally gives us something to do with the Fire from Cauterize, and it's definitely something good.

Meanwhile, the bottom action can theoretically be very powerful against some of the most annoying enemies in the game as enemies with high Shield and low HP typically have flying, allowing you to drop them into traps or at least remove their flying so they can be pushed into one. It's highly situational but also very useful when the situations arises.

Bone Breaker is good but honestly nothing special, especially if you consider what we just got at level 7.

Fortunately, a highly situational bottom is perfectly fine on the other half of a spammable top Attack.

Vengeful Barrage top is terrible. It's meant to be a sort of culmination of the Retaliate build but it's a loss that's legitimately so much worse than our level 4 loss on Flurry. It can waste charges, it often requires being Attacked first, and even considering how you can trigger it yourself, it's just never really worth playing (even on a boss fight where you just want to dump a bunch of damage into a single target, you already have much better tools).

Fortunately, the bottom action here is really good. Obviously we have access to Fire by now and getting a one-round Blood Pact on top of a Move 4 just at the cost of a Fire is very appealing. Unfortunately, it can't be used effectively alongside Burning Hatred, making it not at all play into what we primarily want to be doing. Sure, it can work well with the upcoming level 9 card, but then you're extremely Fire-hungry with Hatred and this while also not having another good Fire producer.

Ultimately, while a powerful effect, given the constraints we're working with, it's just too costly.

We take Bone Breaker because it helps to further enable our best action rather than conflicting with it.

Bone Breaker replaces Shiny Distraction, letting us change a bottom Fire producer into a top one, which is a welcome change. We are currently running two Fire producers and only one Fire consumer, but that's intentional. If we have just one of each, losing either completely breaks our best card (either Burning Hatred without Fire or just losing Burning Hatred). By having two Fire producers, we have redundancy that allows us to lose one, if necessary, and still maintain our combo.

New Cards

Immortality top is legitimately probably worse than the level 1 loss on Defiance of Death, even for the 1-hp build that it's meant to be used in. Sure, for that build you could theoretically use two losses like this, but Maw is legitimately better in that build anyway because of the initiative and AoE Attack (just skip the Heal), so it really doesn't matter. This loss is just embarrassing at level 9.

Meanwhile, the bottom is supposed to be the final non-loss action you need in your Retaliate build. The contrast between this bottom and the top of Maw/Hatred should really serve to highlight all there is to say about that build. Again, it can be done in the ideal party composition, but if you're going to build an ideal party composition around one build, even this class offers a better one (the one-hp build).

Well, at least level 9 finally provides an easy choice on the Berserker.

Even if this is worse than Burning Hatred, it's still a good enough action, and the self-healing will allow you to help offset incoming damage from your occasionally-reckless positioning as well as Blood Pact.

The bottom is also just sort of fine. It's highly situational that you'd want to use this bottom over the top, but obviously it can come up, and then it's there at almost no opportunity cost while on the other half of this card.

Maw of Madness is good, although a little bit of a let-down at level 9 after the insanity of Burning Hatred at level 7, but that's okay.

The best thing about this card is the initiative, allowing you to run in later with Burning Hatred the turn before, then go very early with this top and either finish the enemies off, or get out afterwards.

We take Maw of Madness, not because it's an insane level 9 card in the same vein as Inferno or Long Con, but because it's just much better than the extremely mediocre Immortality (what a shame to use such a cool name on such a poor card).

The Maw of Madness replaces Flurry of Axes. One-shot losses scale poorly in Gloomhaven and while Flurry was very strong for a loss, it's not exception. Our average expected damage on Advantaged Attacks (which is most of our Attacks, and certainly should be all of your Flurry Attacks) hasn't increased since our 6th perk, so the damage here really just isn't scaling up. We've now got Burning Hatred for a similar non-loss effect, as well as Maw, meaning we really just don't need this anymore. And while it was still a good Move card, Strength in Agony provides all the movement we could ever want (obviously you can consider bringing Flurry back in for scenarios where you really have to run a lot, like 'escape' scenarios).
