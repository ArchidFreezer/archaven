Blinkblade

The Blinkblade is a mid-health tactical damage dealer who must choose ‘Fast’ or ‘Slow’ for all of its turns, with attendant bonuses and penalties. ‘Fast’ turns cost a Time Token, and ‘Slow’ turns gain a Time Token, up to 2. Some Blinkblade abilities cost or gain Time Tokens beyond this ‘Fast’ and ‘Slow’ usage. Time Tokens above 2 can NEVER be gained by taking a ‘Slow’ turn, only by other effects. Aside from this, Blinkblades have some minor support options and one of the best movement ability sets we’ve ever seen to ensure they can fight surgically, duck in and out of combat, and accomplish movement-based objectives with ease.

Central to the Blinkblade experience is that you need to fit ‘Slow’ turns in among your powerful ‘Fast’ turns. Use only the initiative and associated ‘Fast’ or ‘Slow’ bonuses on the cards for ‘Fast’ or ‘Slow’ (unless prompted otherwise). (‘Fast’ is left/green, ‘Slow’ is right/yellow). Unless your ability cards or Attack Modifier Deck (your attack cards with +2, -2, etc) grant you a Time Token, you must perform a ‘Slow’ turn to generate a time counter for every ‘Fast’ turn you want to play. Because your ‘Slow’ turns tend to have worse bonuses, maximizing Blinkblade’s output is about figuring out how to get the most out of your ‘Slow’ turns while also putting yourself in position to “turn it up to 11” and go all-out when you can take your 'Fast' turns.

The Blinkblade’s primary role in a party is to focus down and eliminate the right enemies at the right times, using their superior movement and initiative weaving to engage the most effective enemies each round. It’s also important to be sure your superior movement keeps you out of harm’s way, carefully selecting exactly how many hits you take during 'Fast' turns so that you aren’t in too much danger when you’re stuck taking 'Slow' turns or your self-sustain and Regenerate options can keep your health in fighting shape.
