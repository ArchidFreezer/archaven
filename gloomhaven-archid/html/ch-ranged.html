Cragheart Ranged Striker

There are many things that the Cragheart can build around and he can be a confusing character to work with. This build will look to use him as a ranged damage dealer as much as possible, coming into melee only when you're out of ranged attacks or using melee attacks and moving away when enemies have closed on you. Your secondary role is support as you do have some nice amount of healing.

Well, you see, you just don't have any early initiative top ranged attacks so you can't pair this with an early card to place the obstacles when you can still predict what they'll do. And finally, the Earth generation continues to not be a priority as we'll have two cards from level 1 and three cards from level 4 that create Earth all the time.

Avalanche is here to setup Rock Slide and make you appreciate an amazing card even more as you previously thought the Cragheart's obstacle mechanics would be as clunky as this card was.

There are some scenarios early in the campaign where the bottom effect here is powerful enough that you will be happy to work around its shortcomings but you'll get Rock Slide sooner or later and then be happy to never look back.

It does give you an easy-to-setup power spike in the last room of a scenario.

Perfectly acceptable movement.

Backup Ammunition is a core card for this class at any level in a 4p party but the lower enemy count in 2p leads this card to being a little more underwhelming as we level. The biggest reason that lower enemy counts hurt this card is because our most common combo with this card is Massive Boulder and with less enemies it becomes more difficult to really benefit from the splash on two targets.

Using this in the last room also generates some much needed xp for us.

The top is a simple attack 3 range 3 most of the time and that's honestly good enough.

Especially with Backup Ammo and the fact that you don't get many more simple non-loss ranged attacks, you'll be happy to keep this around for that alone.

Because you often have more Earth than you spend, the Earth consumption line on this card will often just amount to 1 xp generation, which is also nice.

Finally, in a not-insignificant number of scenarios, the Push will let you spring traps.

Crater is a very nice roleplayer card - a card that's never really very exciting but has a pretty simple job and does that job well.

It should be mentioned that ranged attacks with Push are pretty rare and are VERY flexible in terms of getting enemies where you need them to be, so when there are high-value traps, this top action will really shine.

Crater, like many Cragheart cards, is really only let down by its initiative (doubly true here as you do need to go before monsters most of the time when you want to use Push effectively).

As with Opposing Strike, an attack 3 isn't great but it's okay, especially at level 1. Immobilize is honestly worse than 1 xp on a melee attack for this class though - you lack the early initiative to move away before monsters go outside of Unstable Upheaval and that card will already be working overtime. Earth generation is core to this class but you almost always have more Earth than you can spend so you honestly don't really ever need to prioritize generation.

Loot 1 bottom actions are quite bad as it's difficult to put yourself in a position where Loot 1 is useful and then use it the following turn - in most of those cases, moving twice will simply yield as many coins. The Earth is nice as you are a real tart for that element.

Crushing Grasp is another mediocre card that we'll use at early levels but will be happy to be rid of soon enough.

Dirt Tornado is absolutely one of the Cragheart's best level 1 actions in a 4p party. 4p scenarios will often have enough enemies that you can consistently get 4-5 targets with this attack which makes the damage very, very reasonable - and the Muddle for mitigation is just a bonus thrown on the top. You also have the possibility of powering this up with either Heaving Swing or Earth, meaning there are multiple routes to getting at least 2 attack on this, at which point the action is definitely good enough. Earth is the more reliable route, however, as while the Heaving Swing combo is excellent (especially on top of Earth), it also has serious initiative issues and initiative is very serious problem on an AoE attack like this for two reasons.

  1. Because you're doing a small amount of damage to many different targets, you typically need your allies to go after you and finish some of the targets you've weakened, otherwise your team is taking a ton of attacks.
  2. If the enemies go before you and move, your great AoE targeting may be ruined.

    3. The bottom is also a perfectly fine move 3 (for this class, at least) for the majority of the time when you don't have ideal targeting.

Dirt Tornado is an excellent card for a Cragheart in a 4p party.

The biggest reason this card is still decent at lower levels is just because it has a reasonable bottom action at the same time.

Earthen Clod's top action is far from exciting but is pretty reasonable. The long range allows you to get guaranteed attacks off when entering a room although the low damage means you'll never be excited to do that. Still, guaranteed attack for 2 is better than a reasonable risk of not being able to make any attack at all and your low movement can make that a very real risk.

Immobilize is also a pretty lacklustre condition but you have enough Earth that you can get it when it will be useful, although once you get Rock Slide at level 4, you'll often find you'd rather just use that any time that Immobilize would do something and just get a significantly more useful action.

While Heal 2 isn't impressive, the decent range means you'll almost always find a relevant target and it will often be used just to remove Poison or Wound, which is quite valuable.

A card that will find some use.

The nature of how the Cragheart plays, especially as he gain levels, means we can easily have a lot of turns where we are able to make effective top actions without needing to move. Accordingly, the few non-move bottom actions we have can accrue a significant amount of value over the course of a scenario and this one has, sadly, some of your more reasonable initiative.

The top action of Forceful Storm is a powerful loss action that can absolutely justify playing the card for a loss. A reasonable amount of damage and crowd control coupled together almost always makes for a good loss and is especially reasonable for a class with 11 cards.

While the bottom is certainly an enticing mechanic to setup some powerful melee combos, you just lack the tools. You don't have much movement to setup the right position and you absolutely don't have the initiative to make sure the combo goes off properly. Later on you'll gain the movement but you'll never have good enough initiative with the attacks you want to perform.

The reason we won't bring Forceful Storm is because we have enough good losses that we don't need another with no other action on it and 53 initiative.

You'll have the option to take a few melee attacks as you level, but you won't get another non-loss 5 damage melee attack until level 7! You also just won't get a ton of effective non-loss attacks period, meaning that even though you'd prefer ranged attacks to melee, this attack will still end up having to do a lot of work for you. And while the attack doesn't completely come online until you hit level 4 and get Rock Slide, the bar for a decent non-loss attack is also lower at level 1-3, so you'll still regularly be happy with it.

The bottom is a very powerful non-move action but just suffers from initiative. You'll most often want to combine this with Dirt Tornado but then you'll be going at 57 initiative which is a problem because you typically want to be the first one to hit enemies when you do a numerous-target AOE so your allies can finish them off before they hit back and if the enemies go first, it can be much more difficult to reliably get as many targets as you would like. The sheer amount of damage you can do with it in 4p means you'll have more incentive to try to get it right (although be careful because you'll also have much greater punishment, in the form of numerous enemies attacking, when it goes wrong).

Heaving Swing is an absolutely core card for the Cragheart in 4p.

Massive Boulder is an incredibly powerful top action compared to other level 1 actions, it really does it all: respectable attack value, especially compared to other level 1 ranged attacks, creates Earth, and does some valuable splash damage.

The bottom is our biggest non-loss move at level 1 but we're still almost never going to play it outside of extremely desperate situations because we never want to lose access to this top action.

Speaking of those cards that create Earth all the time, here's one of them.

The only problem with this card is the initiative but you can't have it all and you are still a Cragheart.

4p parties definitely appreciate having some healing around and by bringing this card along with you (on top of Rumbling Advance and Earthen Clod), you can pretty much remove the need to have another class with significant healing.

The bottom loss on this card is just a lot of text and symbols to tell you never to use it.

Because of the number of allies and enemies on the board at all times in a 4p party, you'll pretty much always be able to find two damaged targets for this, and it's great at removing multiple instances of Wound or Poison, which is one of the best things healing can actually do in Gloomhaven.

The top action of this card isn't going to hit 3 targets, except in some fantasy scenario, but an attack 3 at range 2 is usable and you might get a couple of enemies if you move into position, except you don't get to move quickly!

The bottom loss here is pretty poor. You don't spend that much time in melee, despite your large health pool, and you don't really have much in the way of defensive options, so taking hits really is something you'd rather avoid. More importantly, you also have a few very powerful loss actions, even from level 1, so you'd much rather spend your valuable losses on some other actions.

The top action is still OK. An attack 3 that gives 1 xp isn't anything to write home about and certainly doesn't compare favourably to other melee damage-dealers at level 1, but you're also not a melee damage-dealer - you help the party in ways those simpletons can't even grasp - and gaining experience at level 1 is really important for you. Your experience gain is pretty poor at levels 1-2 and you really want to get to level 4 as quickly as possible to unlock your true potential.

A highly situation top that will be rarely used, but is another way of infusing Earth.

Another situation action on a bottom loss.

Sometimes, it would seem, theme gets in the way of mechanics. There's not really much more I can say about this card: it's pretty much just there if you want to RP a mole. A highly situational top action with a situational bottom loss for a class that has numerous better loss actions.

This card could have at least had good initiative and then it might have competed for Unstable Upheaval's 'Move 2 with 13 initiative' slot but as-is you should pretty much never bring this card unless there's a very, very specific reason relating to cheesing the scenario you're otherwise struggling with.

The top half will rarely be used in combat but it's always very nice to have some healing (and Heal 4 is some very good healing for a level 1 card) around for when you're done with one room and moving to the next, allowing you to recover missing health before the next engagement and avoid having to lose cards. In those cases, the Earth generation can also work nicely on the top as you can set it up to have it as you enter the room, which can be invaluable. As we usually use healing outside of combat, the range limitation is rarely an issue.

Then for when you need to use the card in combat, you've got a move 2 with 29 initiative that creates Earth and gives you some free direct damage, which is certainly invaluable against some very annoying types of enemies.

Rumbling Advance is a really great card with two very nice halves and one of your best initiatives to boot.

I've said that we don't care so much about Earth creation because we have more of it than we use but that's largely because of this card and Massive Boulder which always make Earth for us - accordingly, it's not that we don't appreciate the Earth here, it's just that because we have the Earth here, we don't need it other places.

A powerful top loss but one you rarely want to use before the end of a scenario unless your party can create Earth for you on the first turn before you go.

Luckily we have an early Move 2 with Earth generation, which we will happily use for quite a while.

This card is a good staple with a powerful loss on one half and a spammable action on the other. We will be happy to take this with us throughout any scenario. The movement isn't great, but it generates Earth to power up our other cards.

The top loss is a great addition to our class. While we do already have a bunch of valuable losses, they're all AOE, which leaves us lacking any way to dump longevity in a boss or boss-like fight to deal a large amount of single-target damage quickly. Blunt Force is absolutely priceless on those types of scenarios, which normally are not the Cragheart's strong suit. Sure, one loss won't suddenly transform you into a Scoundrel, but it does help a lot.

The bottom does have one of the worst keywords in the game - Retaliate. This is awful in Gloomhaven for multiple reasons.

So if it's bad, why is this card (and thus obviously this action) actually good? Well, quite simply, because it's a move 2 with 21 initiative that gives 1 XP. XP is really valuable for this class.

21 initiative is, by far, the second-best initiative we can have right now, so that makes this really important. This card will basically be Unstable Upheaval #2, which gives 1 XP each time but goes slightly later. And that's more than good enough! You can, and should, pretty much completely ignore the Retaliate line on this card (at least until you get 100g for enhancements) and you'll be better off for it.

Blunt Force is a card that I didn't really appreciate much at first but the more you use it, the more you'll come to understand how invaluable it truly is.

In 4p, the power ceiling for this card is very high as it can frequently function as non-loss 2-3 target Attack 3. We have more than enough Earth to power this up any time we play it so the problem is just whether the room/enemy spacing allows us to effectively toss obstacles or not.

An alright loss for a class with a ton of better losses and while it can be very useful on a few very specific scenarios, it's not remotely consistent, furthering the variance issues with this card.

Clear the Way is a card that really fluctuates constantly in effectiveness depending on the rooms in a scenario and the types of enemies.

The final problem with Clear the Way is that we don't have good obstacle generation until level 4, meaning this card will be even more scenario-dependent until then. All that being said, we have so many consistently-good cards like Massive Boulder, Rumbling Advance, and Heaving Swing that have two good actions on both halves of those cards that we can often afford to have a higher-upside card hanging out and only getting used half as often. And, in a level, this card will get a LOT better. Finally, the biggest argument for this card is also that we just won't get any more non-loss ranged attacks until level 9, meaning we kind of need this (as ranged attacks are so much better than melee in a 4p party).

The ability to execute Elite enemies unconditionally at range is a powerful loss.

Moving and then Immobilizing is intended to help the melee build, I suppose, but means the condition will rarely do more than Muddle ranged enemies, at best.

Other classes can use this sort of effect to go late and Immobilize then go early the following round, leaving the Immobilized enemies stuck behind, but we typically lack the early initiative on another card to pull that off.

Petrify combines a powerful loss with a spammable bottom. The bottom isn't great, but is usable and we really need some more low initiatives.

Without comboing this card, you're mostly left with a low-damage AOE attack that usually only hits two targets at level 5 and that's absolutely not what you want.

The bottom loss is powerful but unwieldy and almost never worth the setup (compare how much easier it is to use Backup Ammo, which gives you similar results, and is a level 1 card).

Stone Pummel is a pretty mediocre card designed around the 'melee build' for the Cragheart. The problem with that build is that you lack the initiative to make it work consistently. For example, the combo you really want to do with this top action is Forceful Storm, but then you still have 32 initiative on a turn where you don't move, which is really not where you want to be at.

Most effective melee attackers in the game can do so with early initiative, this build for the Cragheart asks you to try to do the same thing only with much worse initiative and the reward is potentially slightly higher damage on some turns.

Overall, I find the inconsistency not to be worth the risk, especially when the Cragheart can still do some incredibly powerful stuff while focusing on more utility/ranged damage.

Blind Destruction is what I call a 'game-breaker', the Gloomhaven version of world-destroyers. Some classes just get level 9 cards (or level 6 cards if you're Eclipse) that trivialize most scenarios in the game. Other starting class examples of this would be the Scoundrel's Long Con and the Spellweaver's Inferno. It's up to you to decide whether you like this sort of mechanic or not - being rewarded for getting to level 9 by getting to do something that feels well and truly broken. Regardless, that is what Blind Destruction does.
