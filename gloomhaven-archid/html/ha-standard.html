Hatchet Standard

This is a fairly standard build for the Hatchet providing consistent ranged damage with very little downside. The weaknesses he has, common to all Jaws of the Lion classes, is a lack of decent movement and average initiative.

He is however very straightforward to play whilst being very effective.

Build Deck

New Cards

Here we get one less Attack than the standard 3, but 2 Healing tacked on, and this is a good deal. Many times with The Favourite, you won't need the full 6, and if you have melee allies, you can toss them a free Heal while you finish off an enemy.

We want the Move 3's we have access to, and this one comes with the best upside yet. A free small Heal that can help top people off but mostly serves as free Wound/Poison removal.

The Earth doesn't matter to us but can be used by an ally, so it is certainly helpful some amount of the time.

Care Package is great, although I still have difficulty visualizing exactly what's being thrown and to whom exactly.

The top feels great to use when it works out, and for all the times that the top doesn't line up, the bottom is obviously great.

An excellent spammable bottom paired with a valuable-if-situational top combines for a card that we're super happy to have.

An Attack 3, Range 3 isn't inherently flashy, but remember that this becomes an Attack 6. Center Mass top will be your most common initial Favourite delivery method when doing single-target damage, which you'll then follow with Attacks that benefit from having The Favourite in the target.

This class really does want Move 3's (as you don't have any non-conditional Move 4's), and this bottom provides one with a reasonable and sometimes useful upside at good enough initiative. At low levels, you'll use the top of this card a lot more than the bottom, but as you level, both halves will become essential.

Center Mass is just an excellent card for this class, with very usable top and bottom actions that we can use at any level. The top scales better than expected because 24 initiative is actually quite good for this class.

Close Cuts top is a great top for the Brute, but as an otherwise entirely-ranged class, it's not very good. You rarely want to get into melee range of two enemies and you can't even add The Favourite to either of the Attacks.

Similarly, the bottom is only good if you start your turn next to an enemy, but you're not a tanky class, so you don't want to let enemies come up and hit you, and you lack late enough initiatives to Move next to an enemy the previous round then do this the following round (and you lack very early initiatives to combine with this, and it's just a lot of work for a small payoff).

So overall, not a good card. That being said, I do think it's worth bringing to scenarios with large numbers of small enemies, like Vermling Scouts. Those enemies die quickly enough that the top is less risky, they don't hit very hard to punish you, and there are enough of them that you'll typically satisfy the targeting condition. But aside from specific scenarios that really want AoE because of enemy types, you're better off leaving this one on the sidelines.

Disorienting Barrage is a pretty good card for this class. The top represents 6 damage across three different enemies which is good value at level 1.

The bottom is a fine loss that will occasionally get used against high-Shield, low-hp enemies, but not a reason to bring the card.

This card is the Hatchet's answer to the pesky high shield, low health enemies.

The top is only our second AoE attack and even though Attack 2 is fairly low one of the targets can be an Attack 5.

Ah, here it is, the boss killer.

Double Throw has a powerful bottom loss with a spammable top which is the way they should be.

We happily play the top until the end of the scenario and then fire off the loss on any big targets in the last room.

Extra Lift is a level 1 card that really wants Wind. We get an Attack 3, Range 5. That seems like it might be better, but in truth, long range isn't necessarily very useful for us because we want to make most of our Attacks with The Favourite or targeting enemies that already have it in them and we typically want to keep The Favourite closer to use to make it easier to get back. That's not to say that long range can't sometimes be useful.

Meanwhile, the bottom is another loss that we're not really in the market for, but it theoretically could be useful for 'escape' or similar scenarios where you need to cover long distances quickly.

So basically, you certainly want either this or Stopping Power and you don't want both. I'm going to be honest with you all, I'm truly not sure which one is better. Personally, I prefer Stopping Power just because an Attack 3, Range 2 is typically going be reasonably better than an Attack 2, Range 3 for when you lose the Wind generator first in the scenario. But some people whose opinions I really respect prefer Extra Lift, so they may be right. I've actually started tracking this while using Stopping Power and across 6 scenarios thus far, there have been two instances where Extra Lift would have been better, but that's also not to say Stopping Power has been better all the rest of the time (I haven't tracked the reverse instances where it was better, sadly).

The top loss is not going to be used on a class that's not naturally tanky, but you might use it once in your Hatchet's lifetime.

Combining this bottom action with Barrage's top gives you an Attack 2, Range 3, Target 3, Wound, which is extremely powerful at low levels, especially at 12 initiative. And, of course, you can throw The Favourite at one of the targets to get the extra damage needed to finish them off. In scenarios where you're immediately in a tough spot in the first room, you'll often want to go with this combo turn 1 over The Favourite, and look to play The Favourite the following turn when you have a bit more breathing room.

Fancy Hat is great, although really as the second part of a one-two punch with Disorienting Barrage, not on its own. The real draw of this card is the bottom and the initiative.

This card also functions fine as a Move 2 with 12 initiative just because this class has overall pretty mediocre initiative.

When you kill an enemy with The Favourite, you can pick it up by Looting the hex where they died, but while The Favourite is in an enemy, retrieving it is much more difficult and significantly less profitable.

Fortunately, you have payoffs like this one, which lets you make a level 1 Attack 4, Range 4, which is pretty incredible.

The bottom + initiative just make the other half a worse version of Center Mass, although as you often need extra movement, it can function as a quasi-Move 4 in a pinch.

Follow Through is an essential card to this class, allowing you to remain effective while The Favourite is stuck in an enemy.

This top loss is pretty solid, honestly. As an effective 10-card class, we can afford a loss on a normal scenario, but considering that you average around an Attack 5 per turn with non-losses, losing turns costs a lot more damage than it gains.

Meanwhile, the bottom is quite good. Move 3 + element is a standard excellent Gloomhaven action when you want the element, and you can certainly use Wind, even if it's not really incredible. Still, it remains a Move 3 (which again, we want) with a not-insignificant upside, so that's good.

We have a top loss with a spammable bottom so we can work with this card.

This loss serves one very clear purpose: to be used on boss fights combined with The Favourite and Double Throw

The 60 initiative is actually your slowest initiative for the time-being (for normal scenarios), so while it's not great for going slow, it's the best you can do and many times you'll give it a try and hope for the best.

You can pretty much just ignore the existence of the top half of this card. I've maaaaybe used it once? You know what's better than going into melee range to make an Attack 2 to get The Favourite back? Making Attack 4's and Attack 5's until the target dies.

The bottom, however, is incredible. First, this action is the biggest reason why this class is a Looting machine. First of all, this lets you both pick up The Favourite but also grab some bonus coins. But the biggest draw here is that you can grab The Favourite and use it in the same turn.

If you Attack before moving, typically you can just use a larger Move to get to the hex where you killed the target and Loot The Favourite at the end of your turn. But if you already have The Favourite lying on the ground, performing this bottom action first still lets you Move a bit to get into range, and then fire off an Attack using The Favourite in the same turn.

This loss pretty much sucks and you'll basically never want to play it, although if you have the card in a scenario, there's a chance you might in dire circumstances. At the end of a scenario it can be used for the 2 XP.

The selling point of this card is another Move 3, which this class wants. The bonus is rarely going to trigger or matter, but it's occasionally there, mostly just for experience.

The reason we want this card is for the Move and good Initiative.

The top is an Attack 3, Range 2 without any requirement, which is alright. And with Wind, which is easy for us to make, it becomes an Attack 3 with a reasonable upside. That being said, we don't often want to Push targets away when we hit them with The Favourite as it makes it harder to retrieve, but with we can just Push 1 to avoid Retaliate, or use this without The Favourite into traps as an alternative method of killing which allows us to not need to recover The Favourite afterwards.

The bottom essentially doesn't exist because again, 10-card class, although it could potentially be used if you were really desperate for melee CC or Jump.

A reasonable card but nothing to get too excited about.

Realistically, you should play this effect in pretty much every scenario, it really does add a ton of damage-on-demand. Obviously, there are some catches to how you use and target with this ability. Getting The Favourite stuck in a high-hp enemy isn't typically a big problem as you have follow-up Attacks that benefit (and give you Attack 4 and Attack 5) although you do need to make sure it's an enemy that you want to prioritize killing because you don't want to have the Favourite stuck in one enemy and then spend your turns attacking somewhere else.

While getting The Favourite stuck in an enemy isn't really dangerous, having it drop to the ground somewhere you can't easily or safely access is. Accordingly, you need to make sure that when you use it, you target enemies in spots you can get to shortly after. Typically, Hatchet focuses down enemies in a front-to-back fashion, or around the edges of the fight. Killing an enemy in the back with The Favourite is a recipe to be ineffective for a number of turns afterwards, especially as the bottom of The Favourite is your only Jump. And again, that's not to say you can't target enemies in difficult-to-reach spots if they're absolute priorities, but in those cases, you should consider holding off on throwing The Favourite unless it's absolutely necessary.

Meanwhile, the bottom is also quite decent with the initiative of this card and is your only non-loss Jump, but it doesn't really matter as you just have to play the top.

The top of this card is your core mechanic, you should absolutely play this persistent loss, you are effectively playing a 10 card class, and other losses need to be evaluated accordingly.

Core

These are our cores cards at level 1, as usual in order of important/power. Admittedly, Retrieval is actually more important than Center Mass as we level, but Center Mass is probably more important at level 1.

Sideboard

And these are our secondary/optional cards. Fancy Hat is part of our AoE combo. At low levels, you'll want to bring this to basically every scenario, but eventually it will fall off unless you're against swarming enemies like Vermling, Hounds, Snakes, etc. Power Pitch makes up our second duo with one of the Wind spenders. Lastly, Second Wind is just fine as an 18 initiative Move 3 given that we have nothing better.

New Cards

The top is a baseline of Attack 3, Range 4, which isn't good for a level 4 card, but it can easily be an Attack 4, Range 5 without much work given the tools we already have available. At the end of the day, as I've already addressed, very long range is less useful for us than most other ranged classes because we don't want to throw The Favourite really far away. Still, we can treat this like Follow Through without needing us to use The Favourite in any way and it's honestly fine.

The bottom is also a decent loss but still pretty mediocre. You want to play this before you enter a new room, but that sort of pre-cast loss on a class with so little longevity is very risky. It can be fine on shorter scenarios though.

The top is highly situational. Mostly, it's just a Loot 1 on a class that really shouldn't be doing any more Looting. The Wound effect is purely there for theme as you really don't want to move up to melee range of an enemy you've hit with The Favourite just to do 2 direct damage and Wound.

In Jaws especially, healing is quite important with the prevalence of Wound and getting an additional mostly-free source is very welcome. Coupled with the movement this bottom will feel really good (and a Heal 3 with an element is a lot better than an Attack 1, especially because the Heal just requires an ally in Range after your top Attack, not an additional enemy, making targeting easier). The element is a small-but-welcome bonus and the Strengthen is mostly irrelevant.

Ripped from the Flesh is also just fine.

The situation where the top is good is when an enemy has The Favourite in them and 2 or less health remaining, which allows you to get a 100% execute while moving up and recover The Favourite. I've had this happen a couple times, but it's reasonably uncommon. The real draw of this card is the bottom action.

I'm actually sort of overselling the card without mentioning the real reason this card is decent: the initiative. Currently, our three fastest initiatives are 12, 24, and 30. Adding a 20 in is a big improvement and the value of early initiative cannot possibly be overemphasized, especially on a class that looks to prevent enemy Attacks by killing them before they go. Thus, the reason we'll be willing to take this otherwise-mediocre card is just because it has a spammable bottom with attractive initiative (literally the best initiative we've been offered at levels 2-4)
