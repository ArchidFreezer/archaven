Quartermaster

As a tank/melee damage-dealer/ranged damage-dealer/item-based support, he is conceptually quite possibly the strangest class in the game, yet it actually all works surprisingly well. In the end what you get is a class that's both very powerful and very versatile, only held in check by his painful 9 card hand limit.

Because of his wide range of abilities, there's not really a good 'way to play him' I can recommend. You have a variety of things you can do all the time and you can't even really build him to do one specific thing. As far as builds go, he doesn't really have defined build paths, the only question is just how much you want to go into his support side vs just damage/tanking. The answer to that will usually depend on your group composition and size. Support, as always, is worse the smaller the group. In a large group, it's pretty simple - if you already have a tank, do damage and support. If you don't have a tank, damage and tank. If you already have a tank and a support (who knows, maybe you were lucky enough to open a box with 5 Quartermasters), a second tank is generally better than a second support. You should almost always prioritize good damage cards over good support cards as damage is the best kind of support, most of the time.
